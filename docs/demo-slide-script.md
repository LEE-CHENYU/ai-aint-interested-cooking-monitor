# Hackathon Demo Slide Script

**Team:** AI Ain't Interested
**Duration:** 5 minutes
**Project:** On-Device AI Kitchen Cooking Monitor

---

## Slide 1: The Problem (30s)

### "Your Kitchen Doesn't Have an AI Copilot — Yet"

- Multitasking in the kitchen is stressful — juggling timers, parallel steps, and safety
- Existing recipe apps show a wall of text. You scroll with greasy hands
- No one watches your pot while you're chopping onions

> **Say:** "Who here has burned pasta because they forgot the timer? Or had a boil-over because they were busy with the sauce? Cooking is fundamentally a multitasking problem — and current tools don't help."

---

## Slide 2: Our Solution (30s)

### "An On-Device AI Cooking Companion"

- Tells you **one step at a time** — no recipe overload
- **Watches your cooking** with a camera and detects when each step is done
- **Alerts you** if something goes wrong (boil-over, smoke)
- Runs **entirely on-device** — no cloud, no latency, no privacy concerns

> **Say:** "We built a cooking companion that runs on a single Gemma model. You tell it a dish — it generates a recipe, guides you step by step, watches your kitchen through a camera, and alerts you when things go wrong. No cloud. No internet. All local."

> **Show:** Phone UI mockup screenshot

---

## Slide 3: Live Demo (2 min)

### "Let's Cook Mapo Tofu"

> **Say:** "Let me show you how it works. I'm going to cook mapo tofu."

**Demo flow:**

1. Start the system — phone shows the dish name and Step 1
   > "The system generated a 6-step recipe. But the cook only sees one step at a time."

2. Step 1: "Dice the tofu" — camera detects diced tofu → auto-advances
   > "The VLM recognized the diced tofu on the cutting board, so it automatically moves on."

3. Step 2: "Mince garlic" — user taps Done button
   > "For steps the camera can't reliably detect, like mincing, the cook just taps Done."

4. Step 3: "Heat oil until shimmering" — VLM detects it → advances
   > "Back to vision detection — it sees the oil is hot."

5. Step 4: timer starts (30 seconds) — countdown on phone
   > "Now we have a timed step. The timer runs and the phone shows the countdown."

6. Safety alert interrupts — boil-over warning flashes red
   > "And if something goes wrong — like a boil-over — the system interrupts with a safety alert, regardless of which step you're on."

7. Recipe completes — celebration screen
   > "And that's it — mapo tofu is done!"

## Slide 4: How It Works (1 min)

### "Architecture: Thin Agent, Thick Rules"

```
User: "mapo tofu"
  → LLM generates recipe (once, cached)
  → Agent loop: Image → VLM → State → Rules → UI
```

- **Gemma 3 4B VLM**, fine-tuned with QLoRA on 36 synthetic kitchen images
- **LLM generates the recipe once** and caches it as YAML — no LLM call per cycle
- **Rule-based step engine** decides transitions — fast, reliable, debuggable
- **3 completion types** designed around model strengths:
  - VLM detection (for what the camera can see)
  - Timer (for timed cooking phases)
  - User confirm (for what the camera can't detect)

> **Say:** "The key insight is: use the LLM where it shines — recipe knowledge and visual understanding — and use deterministic rules for everything else. This keeps the loop fast and predictable."
