[0:00] START
  "Let me show you how the system works end-to-end.
   I'm going to cook mapo tofu."

[0:05] STEP 1 — Dice tofu (VLM detection)
  "The system generated a 6-step recipe, but the cook only
   sees one step at a time — no recipe overload.

   On the right, you see the kitchen camera feed. Our fine-tuned
   Gemma 3 4B VLM analyzes every frame. Right now it sees
   uncut tofu on the cutting board — step_complete is false."

  >> Pause. Let the audience read the VLM output.

  "Watch what happens when the tofu is diced..."

[0:50] STEP 1 COMPLETES — VLM auto-advances
  "The VLM detected diced tofu — step_complete flipped to true.
   The phone automatically advanced to Step 2. No button press.
   No timer. The camera decided."

[1:05] STEP 2 — Mince aromatics (user confirm)
  "Now, mincing garlic and ginger is hard for a camera to detect
   reliably. So the system shows a Done button instead.

   This is one of our three completion types:
   - VLM detection for what the camera CAN see
   - Timer for cooking phases
   - User confirm for everything else

   The cook taps Done when they're finished."

[1:45] STEP 3 — Heat oil (VLM detection)
  "Back to vision detection. The VLM is looking for
   shimmering oil in the wok.

   It says 'Oil not hot yet' — step_complete is false.
   We wait..."

[2:10] STEP 3 COMPLETES
  "There it is — 'Oil shimmering in wok.' Auto-advance."

[2:25] STEP 4 — Doubanjiang (timer)
  "Now a timed step: cook the doubanjiang paste for 30 seconds.
   Watch the countdown ring on the phone — it ticks down live.

   The timer runs independently. The cook doesn't need to
   set anything — the system knows this step has a duration."

  >> Let the timer tick down visibly for ~20 seconds.

[2:50] SAFETY ALERT — Boil-over!
  "Now here's the critical feature. While we're in Step 4,
   the camera ALSO monitors for safety hazards continuously.

   The VLM just detected a boil-over — safe is false,
   'Liquid spilling over rim.'

   The phone immediately shows a red safety alert:
   'Boil-over detected! Reduce heat immediately!'

   This happens regardless of which step you're on.
   Safety monitoring never sleeps."

  >> Let the alert sit on screen. Let it sink in.

[3:15] STEP 5 — Simmer (timer)
  "Crisis averted. Back to cooking.

   Add tofu and broth, simmer for 5 minutes. Another timed step.
   The countdown runs on the phone. The cook can walk away —
   when it's done, they'll hear an audio notification."

  >> Let the timer tick down for ~40 seconds.

[4:00] STEP 6 — Cornstarch (VLM detection)
  "Final step. Add cornstarch slurry and stir.

   The VLM is looking for a thick, glossy sauce —
   that's how it knows this step is complete.

   And there it is: 'Thick glossy sauce,' step_complete = true."

[4:30] DONE!
  "Mapo tofu is done! The phone shows a celebration screen.

   Six steps. Three completion types. One safety interrupt.
   All driven by a single fine-tuned Gemma 3 4B model
   running entirely on-device."

[4:55] WRAP
  "No cloud. No internet. No privacy concerns.
   Just an AI copilot that watches, guides, and protects."

[5:00] END


================================================================
  QUICK REFERENCE — KEY TALKING POINTS

If asked about the model:
  "Gemma 3 4B, fine-tuned with QLoRA on 36 synthetic images.
   Training took 6 minutes 45 seconds on 8.5 GB VRAM.
   We only trained 0.69% of parameters."

If asked about accuracy:
  "State classification went from 25% to 61% after fine-tuning.
   Water-based boil-over detection hit 100%.
   With more training data, these numbers go higher."

If asked about completion types:
  "Three types, designed around what the model is good at:
   1. VLM detection — for things the camera can see
   2. Timer — for cooking phases with known duration
   3. User confirm — for steps the camera can't judge"

If asked about safety:
  "Safety monitoring runs every inference cycle, independent
   of the current step. It never pauses. Boil-over detection
   is our strongest signal at 100% for water-based dishes."

If asked about latency:
  "After merging LoRA weights into the base model,
   inference runs at native speed — roughly 3-5 seconds
   per frame on consumer hardware."
